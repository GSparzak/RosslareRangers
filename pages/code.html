CODE OF RESPECT

  • Players
  • Coaches
  • Parents

Players should:

  • Always show respect for team-mates, managers/coaches, opponents and officials.
  • Always turn up on time for matches and training.
  • Always play to win but accept defeat like proper sportsmen.
  • Always have commitment to your club and pride in your performance.
  • Ensure that your lifestyle helps rather than hinders your progress.
  • Pay your subs when due.
  • Listen to advice given and ask questions if you don’t understand.
  • Help out with equipment before and after matches and training.
  • Let the manager know, as soon as possible, if you are injured or sick.
  • Bullying will not be tolerated and if you feel you are being bullied feel safe to tell the manager or your parents/guardian.
  • Respect the kit and facilities offered by the club and return all club equipment upon leaving the club.
  • Enjoy playing and training with Rosslare Rangers AFC.

Coaches should:

  • Managers/Coaches or any person or persons representing the club in any capacity will show respect for all players and officials and lead by example.
  • Be fully prepared for coaching sessions and matches.
  • Ensure a safe environment for learning and development.
  • Encourage each player in terms of self-discipline, good behaviour and punctuality.
  • Be self-reflective and not afraid to seek assistance.
  • Liaise with parents.
  • Ensure that all players are aware that all forms of bullying will not be tolerated.
  • Be generous with your praise when it is deserved.
  • Managers/Coaches or any person or persons representing the club in any capacity must always display high standards of behaviour and appearance.
  • Adhere to all rules set out by the FAI, SFAI, Wexford Football League and the club.

Parents should:

  • Always show respect for all players, opponents, managers/coaches and officials.
  • Have realistic expectations for your son/daughter and don’t put him/her under undue pressure.
  • Ensure that your son/daughter arrives on time for training and matches and that safe arrangements are in place for getting your son/daughter home afterwards.
  • Attend matches and support Rosslare Rangers AFC activities whenever possible.
  • Leave coaching to the coaches.
  • Respect the managers/coaches decisions.
  • Assist when asked.
  • Accept disappointment, praise good work and refrain from criticising.
  • Support the club’s fund-raising activities where possible.
  • If your son/daughter requires hospital treatment a parent must attend.
  • Emphasise skill development and practice over winning and how they benefit your child (and other children/players). De-emphasise games and competition in the lower age groups.
  • As you may be aware the Managers, Coaches and committee members are volunteers and have lives outside the Club.Therefore any queries you may have if they could be addressed within RRAFC days and times.
